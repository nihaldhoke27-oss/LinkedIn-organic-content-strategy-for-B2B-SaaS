# B2B SaaS Content Frameworks

A comprehensive reference library of the most important content frameworks used by B2B SaaS companies for LinkedIn organic growth and broader content strategy.

---

## Framework 1: The Content Pillar System

**Source:** Justin Welsh (adapted widely across the industry)

**What it is:** A method for organizing all LinkedIn content around 3–5 core topic areas ("pillars") that define your expertise and attract your ideal audience.

**How it works:**

**Step 1 — Define your pillars**
Choose 3–5 topics that:
- You have genuine expertise in
- Your target audience cares about
- Are specific enough to build authority (not "marketing" — instead "LinkedIn content strategy for Series A SaaS founders")

**Example pillars for a SaaS founder:**
1. LinkedIn organic growth for B2B founders
2. Building a remote-first sales team
3. SaaS pricing strategy
4. Founder mental health and resilience
5. Product-led growth for early-stage SaaS

**Step 2 — Create content within pillars**
Every post should belong to one pillar. This consistency:
- Signals to the algorithm what your account is "about"
- Trains your audience to expect certain topics from you
- Makes content batching dramatically easier

**Step 3 — Rotate pillars over a week**
A sample weekly rotation:
- Monday: Pillar 1 (LinkedIn growth)
- Tuesday: Pillar 4 (founder mental health)
- Wednesday: Pillar 2 (sales team building)
- Thursday: Pillar 3 (SaaS pricing)
- Friday: Pillar 5 (PLG) or personal story crossing multiple pillars

## Framework 2: Hook → Teach → Ask (HTA)

**Source:** Justin Welsh

**What it is:** The three-part structure that governs every high-performing LinkedIn post.

**The components:**

**Hook (Line 1):** The single sentence that determines whether anyone reads the rest. Must stop the scroll, create curiosity or tension, and make a clear promise.

Hook formula options:
- Contrarian claim: "Most [common belief] is wrong. Here's the truth:"
- Specific number: "I [did X thing] for [time period]. Here's what happened:"
- Personal transformation: "2 years ago [negative situation]. Today [positive outcome]. The difference:"
- Question: "What if [common assumption] is actually [counterintuitive conclusion]?"

**Teach (Lines 2–8):** The body of the post. Delivers the promised value with:
- Short paragraphs (max 2 sentences)
- Specific examples, not generic advice
- Numbered or bulleted lists for complex information
- White space between every paragraph

**Ask (Final Line):** The call to action. One specific ask:
- Follow: "Follow me for more frameworks like this."
- Comment: "Which of these do you use? Comment below."
- DM: "DM me [keyword] for the full template."
- Link: "Full breakdown in the first comment."

## Framework 3: Create Once, Distribute Forever (CODF)

**Source:** Ross Simmonds / Foundation Marketing

**What it is:** A content distribution philosophy that maximizes ROI from each piece of content by distributing it across 10+ channels over 12+ months.

**The 12-Channel Distribution Map:**

| # | Channel | Content Format | Timeline |
|---|---------|---------------|----------|
| 1 | LinkedIn (Personal) | 3 text posts + 1 carousel | Week 1, 4, 8 |
| 2 | LinkedIn (Company Page) | 2 posts | Week 1, 3 |
| 3 | Twitter/X | Thread | Week 1 |
| 4 | Newsletter | Featured section + link | Week 2 |
| 5 | Podcast | Episode discussion | Week 3 |
| 6 | YouTube | Video version | Week 4 |
| 7 | Blog post | Long-form written version | Week 1 |
| 8 | Quora | Answer with citation | Week 2, ongoing |
| 9 | Reddit | Post in relevant subreddit | Week 2 |
| 10 | SlideShare | Deck version | Week 3 |
| 11 | Sales enablement | SDR/AE reference | Month 2+ |
| 12 | Community | Slack/Discord channels | Week 2 |

**Implementation rule:** Every piece of content should have a written 12-channel distribution plan before it is published.

## Framework 4: The Demand Generation Flywheel

**Source:** Chris Walker / Refine Labs

**What it is:** A model for how LinkedIn organic content creates compounding demand in B2B SaaS — driving pipeline through dark social and brand awareness rather than direct lead capture.

**The flywheel stages:**

```
LinkedIn content (daily) 
→ builds brand awareness in your ICP
→ ICP shares content in dark social (Slack, DMs, email)
→ referral traffic arrives at your website (shows as "Direct")
→ inbound pipeline increases
→ success enables more content investment
→ flywheel accelerates
```

**Measurement protocol:**
1. Self-reported attribution question on demo forms: "How did you first hear about us?"
2. Pipeline interviews with won customers: "What content touched you before booking a call?"
3. Brand search volume tracking (Google Search Console, Ahrefs)
4. Inbound pipeline trend (month-over-month, correlated with content publishing volume)

## Framework 5: The B2B Brand Voice Framework

**Source:** Dave Gerhardt / Exit Five

**What it is:** A set of criteria for evaluating whether B2B SaaS content sounds like a human being or a corporate committee.

**The Brand Voice Test — 5 Questions:**

1. **Contractions test:** Does the content use contractions (it's, we're, you'll)? If it sounds formal and stiff, rewrite.
2. **Position test:** Does the content take a clear side? Or does it hedge with "it depends"? Great brand voice picks a side.
3. **Specificity test:** Does the content include specific examples, numbers, or stories? Or does it make vague claims? Be specific.
4. **Uniqueness test:** Could this content have been published by any competitor? If yes, rewrite until only your company could have published it.
5. **Read-aloud test:** Read the post aloud. Does it sound like something a human being would say in a meeting or phone call?

**The Brand Voice Persona Framework:**
Define your B2B brand voice with a 2×2 matrix:
- X-axis: Formal ←→ Casual
- Y-axis: Serious ←→ Playful

Position your brand in one quadrant and ensure all content is created within that quadrant.

## Framework 6: The Show Me You Know Me (SMYKM) Framework

**Source:** Sam McKenna / #samsales

**What it is:** A personalization framework for B2B LinkedIn outreach that dramatically increases reply rates by demonstrating genuine research.

**The 5-point research checklist:**
1. Activity tab review (2 min): What has the prospect been engaging with recently?
2. Post history review (2 min): What has the prospect been publishing?
3. About section (1 min): What mission, values, and career trajectory do they emphasize?
4. Shared connections (1 min): Who do you know in common?
5. Company news (3 min): What has happened at their company in the last 90 days?

**The SMYKM message formula:**
```
[Specific observation about their situation] + 
[Genuine insight connecting to that observation] + 
[One low-friction question or offer]
```

## Framework 7: The 5-Layer Authenticity Framework

**Source:** Amelia Sordell / Klowt

**What it is:** A framework for deciding which level of personal authenticity to deploy in LinkedIn content, from safest to most differentiated.

| Layer | Type | Example | Differentiation |
|-------|------|---------|----------------|
| 1 | Professional wins | "We just closed our biggest deal" | Low |
| 2 | Professional learnings | "Here's what we learned from our biggest deal" | Medium-low |
| 3 | Business challenges | "Our worst quarter: the honest breakdown" | Medium-high |
| 4 | Personal struggles linked to business | "Burnout hit me: here's what I'm changing" | High |
| 5 | Radical vulnerability | "I almost shut the business down" | Very high |

**Key insight:** Most B2B founders only use Layers 1–2. Moving to Layers 3–4 creates dramatic differentiation and engagement uplift.

## Framework 8: The Specificity Ladder

**Source:** Lara Acosta

**What it is:** A framework for progressively narrowing your LinkedIn niche to increase the magnetizing power of your content.

**The Ladder:**

| Rung | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | General topic | "I write about marketing" |
| 2 | Industry-specific | "I write about B2B marketing" |
| 3 | Role-specific | "I write about B2B SaaS marketing" |
| 4 | Function-specific | "I write about LinkedIn organic content for B2B SaaS" |
| 5 | Outcome-specific | "I help B2B SaaS founders generate qualified pipeline from LinkedIn organic content" |

**Rule:** Keep climbing the ladder until the description is so specific that only your exact target audience would resonate with it. Generic content attracts generic audiences. Specific content attracts the right audience.

**What it is:** A post-type rotation system ensuring variety across different content goals (reach, trust, conversion).

## Framework 10: The Revenue Vitals Measurement System

**Source:** Chris Walker / Refine Labs

**What it is:** A set of leading and lagging indicators for measuring the business impact of LinkedIn organic content in B2B SaaS.

**Leading indicators (measure monthly):**
- LinkedIn follower growth rate
- Content engagement rate (comments + reposts ÷ impressions)
- Email newsletter open rate and growth
- Brand search volume (Google Search Console)
- Inbound DM volume

**Lagging indicators (measure quarterly):**
- Inbound pipeline volume (correlated with content investment)
- Pipeline close rate for inbound leads
- Deal velocity (time-to-close for content-influenced deals)
- Customer acquisition cost (trend line)
- Self-reported attribution to LinkedIn

**Implementation:** Create a monthly "LinkedIn Dashboard" in Google Sheets or Notion tracking all 10 metrics. Review quarterly to identify content investment vs. pipeline correlation.
