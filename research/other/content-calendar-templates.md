# LinkedIn Content Calendar Templates

Ready-to-use content calendar templates for B2B SaaS founders and marketing teams building a LinkedIn organic content engine.

---

## Template 1: Weekly Content Calendar (Founder)

For B2B SaaS founders posting 5×/week on LinkedIn. Uses a rotating archetype system to ensure variety, consistency, and comprehensive audience journey coverage.

---

### Weekly Post Type Rotation

| Day | Post Type | Content Focus | Goal |
|-----|-----------|---------------|------|
| Monday | Framework/Educational | Teach a system, process, or methodology relevant to your ICP | Establish expertise |
| Tuesday | Personal Story | Share a business experience — challenge, lesson, or transformation | Build emotional connection |
| Wednesday | Hot Take / Opinion | Contrarian perspective on a trend in your industry | Drive comments and reach |
| Thursday | Social Proof / Case Study | Client win, testimonial, or data-backed result | Build trust and conversion intent |
| Friday | Question / Community | Ask your audience a relevant question | Build engagement and loyalty |

---

### Monthly Theme System

Plan content around one theme per month aligned to your ICP's seasonal priorities:

| Month | Theme Example (B2B SaaS) | Post Focus |
|-------|--------------------------|------------|
| January | New year GTM planning | Content strategy, goal-setting frameworks |
| February | Pipeline building | LinkedIn outreach, demand gen tactics |
| March | Q1 review | Analyzing what's working, pivoting what isn't |
| April | Content scaling | Systems for consistent content production |
| May | Community and brand | Building audience loyalty, community-led growth |
| June | Mid-year audit | Content ROI measurement, attribution |
| July | Founder stories | Behind-the-scenes content, summer engagement |
| August | Seasonal adjustment | Lighter content, evergreen repurposing |
| September | Fall GTM reset | New strategies for Q4, hiring, team content |
| October | Conversion optimization | LinkedIn profile audit, CTA testing |
| November | End-of-year planning | Content retrospective, 2026 planning |
| December | Reflection and preview | Year in review, goals for next year |

---

### Weekly Engagement Protocol

In addition to posting, schedule daily engagement time:

| Day | Activity | Time |
|-----|----------|------|
| Monday | Comment on 10 ICP posts | 15 min |
| Tuesday | Reply to all post comments | 10 min |
| Wednesday | DM 3 engaged followers | 15 min |
| Thursday | Comment on 5 peer creator posts | 10 min |
| Friday | Review analytics; plan next week | 20 min |

## Template 2: 90-Day LinkedIn Content Sprint

For founders or teams starting from zero or relaunching their LinkedIn presence. Designed to establish algorithmic momentum and audience trust.

---

### Sprint Goals

| Week | Followers Goal | Engagement Goal | Business Goal |
|------|---------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Week 1–2 | +50 | First comments | Profile optimized |
| Week 3–4 | +200 | 10+ comments/post | First inbound DM |
| Week 5–8 | +500 | 20+ comments/post | 3+ qualified conversations |
| Week 9–12 | +1,000 | Consistent >3% engagement | 1+ discovery call booked |

---

### Week-by-Week Content Plan

**Weeks 1–2: Foundation (Profile + First Posts)**

Profile tasks:
- [ ] Optimize LinkedIn headline
- [ ] Rewrite About section for ICP
- [ ] Update Featured section with lead magnet
- [ ] Enable Creator Mode

Content plan:
- 3 posts/week
- Post type: Introduction + Framework + Story
- Theme: Establish who you are and what you teach

Sample Week 1 posts:
- Day 1 (Mon): Introduction post — who you are and who you help
- Day 3 (Wed): Framework post — your #1 framework for [ICP's top challenge]
- Day 5 (Fri): Story post — the experience that led you to this work

**Weeks 3–4: Building (Consistency + Engagement)**

Content plan:
- 4 posts/week
- Post types: Framework, Story, Hot Take, Social Proof
- Engagement: 20 comments/day on ICP content

Sample Week 3 posts:
- Day 1 (Mon): Framework — your process for [common ICP task]
- Day 2 (Tue): Story — a challenge you faced and overcame
- Day 4 (Thu): Hot take — your contrarian view on a common practice
- Day 5 (Fri): Social proof — a client result (anonymized if needed)

**Weeks 5–8: Momentum (Format Testing + Lead Generation)**

Content plan:
- 5 posts/week
- Introduce 1 carousel post per week
- Add DM follow-up with every engaged follower (twice or more)
- Launch email list / newsletter if not already done

**Weeks 9–12: Optimization (Data-Driven Doubling Down)**

Analytics review:
- Which 3 posts generated the most DMs? → Repeat that format/topic
- Which 3 posts generated the most comments? → Repeat that hook style
- Which post generated the most profile visits? → Make a series of that type

Content plan:
- 5 posts/week
- Double down on top-performing formats
- Introduce 1 new format (video, poll, or LinkedIn newsletter)

### Monthly Content Audit Questions

Answer these 5 questions monthly to continuously improve:

1. **What was my top-performing post this month?** (By DMs, comments, or profile visits — not likes)

2. **What format did it use?** (Text, carousel, video, poll)

3. **What topic did it cover?** (Which of my content pillars?)

4. **What hook did it use?** (Story, framework, hot take, social proof, question)

5. **How can I create 3 more posts like this next month?**

## Template 5: Post Batch Writing Template

Use this template during your weekly 2-hour content batching session.

---

### Post Template (Hook → Teach → Ask)

```markdown
---
POST #[number] | PILLAR: [1/2/3/4] | TYPE: [Framework/Story/Hot Take/Social Proof/Question]
DATE TO PUBLISH: [Monday/Tuesday/etc]
HOOK TYPE: [Contrarian / Number / Transformation / Question]
---

HOOK (1 line):
[Write your opening line here — stop the scroll]

BODY (3–7 short paragraphs):
[Paragraph 1 — expand on the hook]

[Paragraph 2 — evidence or context]

[Paragraph 3 — step 1 or key insight 1]

[Paragraph 4 — step 2 or key insight 2]

[Paragraph 5 — step 3 or key insight 3]

[Optional paragraph 6 — result or proof]

CLOSE (1 line):
[Summary of the core lesson]

CTA (1 line):
[Follow / Comment / DM / Link in first comment]

---
FIRST COMMENT (if link needed):
[URL to resource, newsletter, or related content]
---
```
