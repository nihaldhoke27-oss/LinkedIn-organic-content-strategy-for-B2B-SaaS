# Books, Podcasts & Additional Resources

A curated reference list of books, podcasts, newsletters, communities, and tools relevant to LinkedIn organic content strategy for B2B SaaS.

---

## Essential Books

### 1. Jab, Jab, Jab, Right Hook — Gary Vaynerchuk
**Buy:** [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Jab-Right-Hook-Tell-Social/dp/006227306X)
**Relevance:** The foundational framework for give-give-give-ask content strategy. The "jab" = value-giving content. The "right hook" = the ask (CTA). Directly applicable to LinkedIn content cadence for B2B founders.
**Key lesson:** Give value relentlessly before asking for anything. The ratio of value-giving to asking determines long-term audience loyalty.

### 2. Building a StoryBrand — Donald Miller
**Buy:** [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Building-StoryBrand-Clarify-Message-Customers/dp/0718033329)
**Relevance:** Clarifies how to frame your B2B SaaS messaging so that the customer is the hero — not your company. Directly applicable to LinkedIn content that attracts buyers rather than alienating them.
**Key lesson:** Position your customer as the hero, your product as the guide. All great B2B content follows this structure.

### 3. Obviously Awesome — April Dunford
**Buy:** [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Obviously-Awesome-Product-Positioning-Customers/dp/1999529065)
**Relevance:** The definitive book on B2B SaaS product positioning. Positioning determines what LinkedIn content to create — your content should reinforce your positioning, not undermine it.
**Key lesson:** Great positioning makes all marketing easier — including LinkedIn content. Start with positioning, then write content.

### 4. Traction — Gabriel Weinberg & Justin Mares
**Buy:** [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Traction-Startup-Achieve-Explosive-Customer/dp/1591848369)
**Relevance:** Covers 19 customer acquisition channels including content marketing and social media. Provides the framework for testing LinkedIn organic as a traction channel alongside paid, SEO, and community.
**Key lesson:** Systematically test LinkedIn organic as one of 19 traction channels — then double down on the 2–3 that show the highest ROI.

### 5. Contagious — Jonah Berger
**Buy:** [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Contagious-Things-Catch-Jonah-Berger/dp/1451686579)
**Relevance:** Explains the science behind why content spreads. The STEPPS framework (Social Currency, Triggers, Emotion, Public, Practical Value, Stories) directly applies to LinkedIn content that gets shared and commented on.
**Key lesson:** Content spreads when it gives people social currency, triggers emotion, and provides practical value — design B2B LinkedIn posts with these drivers.

### 6. This Is Marketing — Seth Godin
**Buy:** [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/This-Marketing-Cant-Until-Learn/dp/0525540830)
**Relevance:** Godin's philosophy of "smallest viable audience" maps directly to the specificity-first approach for LinkedIn content. Argues for finding the smallest group that could change everything, not marketing to everyone.
**Key lesson:** LinkedIn content that resonates with 1,000 exactly right people is more valuable than content that vaguely interests 100,000.

### 7. Demand-Side Sales 101 — Bob Moesta
**Buy:** [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Demand-Side-Sales-101-Customer-Progress/dp/1544509987)
**Relevance:** Introduces Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD) thinking for B2B sales. Deeply relevant for creating LinkedIn content that speaks to the "jobs" your buyers are trying to get done — not just the features of your product.
**Key lesson:** Write LinkedIn content about the buyer's struggle and desired outcome, not about your product's capabilities.

## Essential Podcasts

### 1. Revenue Vitals (Chris Walker / Refine Labs)
**URL:** https://www.refinelabs.com/podcast
**Also available:** Spotify, Apple Podcasts, YouTube
**Why listen:** The most rigorous podcast on B2B demand generation and LinkedIn content strategy. Episodes cover dark social, attribution, content-led pipeline, and founder-led marketing with genuine data and first-principles thinking.
**Best episodes to start with:** Any episode on LinkedIn organic content, dark social attribution, or demand gen vs. lead gen.

### 2. Exit Five Podcast (Dave Gerhardt)
**URL:** https://www.exitfive.com/podcast
**Also available:** Spotify, Apple Podcasts, YouTube
**Why listen:** Interviews with B2B SaaS CMOs on brand building, content strategy, and LinkedIn. Demonstrates great B2B marketing thinking in action — every episode is a case study in the anti-corporate brand voice Dave teaches.
**Best episodes to start with:** Episodes with CMOs from high-growth SaaS companies discussing their LinkedIn and content strategy.

### 3. The Marketing Millennials (Daniel Murray)
**URL:** https://www.themarketingmillennials.com/podcast
**Also available:** Spotify, Apple Podcasts, YouTube
**Why listen:** Broad coverage of B2B content marketing, LinkedIn tactics, and community-led growth. Very practical and fast-moving — great for specific tactical takeaways.
**Best episodes to start with:** Episodes on LinkedIn algorithm, newsletter building, and B2B media brand strategy.

### 4. The B2B Growth Podcast (Sweet Fish Media)
**URL:** https://sweetfishmedia.com/b2b-growth/
**Also available:** Spotify, Apple Podcasts
**Why listen:** Long-running podcast covering B2B content strategy, account-based marketing, and SaaS growth. Relevant for understanding the broader B2B content landscape around LinkedIn strategy.

### 5. Marketing Against the Grain (Kipp Bodnar & Kieran Flanagan — HubSpot)
**URL:** https://www.hubspot.com/podcast-network/marketing-against-the-grain
**Also available:** Spotify, Apple Podcasts
**Why listen:** Two HubSpot marketing leaders sharing counterintuitive marketing takes. Relevant for B2B SaaS teams using LinkedIn content as part of a broader inbound strategy.

### 6. Lenny's Podcast (Lenny Rachitsky)
**URL:** https://www.lennyspodcast.com
**Also available:** Spotify, Apple Podcasts, YouTube
**Why listen:** Focused on B2B SaaS product and growth. Many episodes cover content-led growth, founder personal branding, and LinkedIn as a GTM channel for product companies.
