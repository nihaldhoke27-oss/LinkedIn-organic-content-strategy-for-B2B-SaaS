## Short Bio

Sam McKenna is the founder of #samsales (also known as #SellBetter), a B2B sales coaching and consulting company. A former VP of Sales at LinkedIn and SNAP, she is renowned for her "Show Me You Know Me" (SMYKM) methodology — a framework for personalizing LinkedIn outreach and content based on genuine research into the prospect's world. Her LinkedIn presence bridges sales and marketing in a unique way: she teaches salespeople and founders how to use LinkedIn content as a relationship-building tool, not a cold outreach channel.

## Why Selected

Sam McKenna uniquely covers the sales side of LinkedIn organic content — a perspective often missing from marketing-focused LinkedIn strategy discussions. For B2B SaaS companies, where AE and SDR personal brands can support pipeline alongside company marketing content, Sam's frameworks are essential. She demonstrates that LinkedIn organic content is not just a marketing tool — it's a sales tool.

## Writing Patterns & Style Analysis

### Hook Style
Sam uses warm, mentor-like openers that feel like insider tips:
- **Insider reveal:** "I spent 10 years at LinkedIn. Here's the prospecting secret nobody outside the company knows:"
- **Tactical tip:** "The one email subject line that gets VPs of Sales to reply every time:"
- **Story hook:** "I once cold-emailed a prospect who became a $2M account. Here's the email."
- **Validation hook:** "If your cold emails have a <5% reply rate, this is why."

### Post Structure
Sam's posts follow a "teaching moment" format:
1. **Experience/credibility anchor** (years at LinkedIn, VP background)
2. **Tactical insight** (the specific thing that works)
3. **Example** (real sales scenario, often anonymized)
4. **Result** (the outcome this tactic produced)
5. **Takeaway** (one sentence that summarizes the lesson)
6. **CTA** (follow, DM, or newsletter link)

### Formatting Habits
- Uses "Here's what I mean:" frequently as a pivot
- Includes specific dialogue examples (sample emails, DM scripts, call openers)
- Uses emojis moderately — adds energy without overwhelming
- Medium-length posts — more than quick takes, less than essays
- Uses real client examples (anonymized) for social proof

### CTA Style
- **Email/DM scripts CTAs:** "Want the full email template? DM me 'EMAIL' and I'll send it."
- **Newsletter CTAs:** "Join 25K+ sellers getting weekly tactics. Link in comments."
- **Follow CTAs:** "Follow me for daily sales tips that actually work in 2025."
- **Community CTAs:** "Join the #SellBetter community — link in bio."

## Recent LinkedIn Post Summaries

> ⚠️ **Note:** LinkedIn does not allow public post scraping. Summaries are based on publicly referenced posts, #samsales newsletter, YouTube content, and widely shared posts.

### Post 1 — Show Me You Know Me (SMYKM) Framework
**Topic:** Personalized LinkedIn outreach
**Summary:** Sam introduces her most famous framework: every piece of outreach (LinkedIn DM, cold email, voicemail) must contain evidence that you've researched this specific person — not just their company. She provides a framework: research the person's (1) recent LinkedIn activity, (2) company news, (3) personal interests from their profile, then weave all three into a one-paragraph opener. Conversion rates vs. generic templates: 40%+ vs. 3%.
**Key Takeaway:** "Show Me You Know Me" — 3 signals of personalization in every outreach = 10× reply rate.
**Hook:** "The cold email that gets VPs to reply has one thing generic emails don't: evidence you did your homework."

### Post 2 — Why SDRs Should Be on LinkedIn Every Day
**Topic:** LinkedIn as a daily sales tool
**Summary:** Sam argues that SDRs who are active on LinkedIn daily (posting content, commenting on prospect posts, sharing insights) warm their own cold outreach by 50%+. When a prospect has seen your name and content three times before your cold email arrives, you're not cold anymore. She shares the 20-minute daily LinkedIn routine she teaches new sales reps.
**Key Takeaway:** Your LinkedIn presence warms your cold outreach before you hit send.

### Post 3 — The LinkedIn Research Checklist Before Every Sales Call
**Topic:** Pre-call LinkedIn research process
**Summary:** Sam shares the 5-point LinkedIn research checklist she developed during her time at LinkedIn: (1) Check recent posts for conversation starters, (2) Look at their connections for mutual contacts, (3) Read their About section for personal mission clues, (4) Review their activity tab for recent comments and shares, (5) Note any shared interests or causes. This research becomes the foundation of the first 2 minutes of every sales call.
**Key Takeaway:** 10 minutes of LinkedIn research before every call converts sales calls from pitches to conversations.

### Post 4 — The LinkedIn Profile That Closes Deals
**Topic:** Sales rep profile optimization
**Summary:** Sam shares how B2B sales reps should optimize their LinkedIn profiles — not for job applications, but for buyer trust. Key changes: (1) headline = who you help + how, not job title, (2) Featured = one client success story, (3) About = written from the buyer's perspective, (4) Skills = keywords buyers search for, (5) Recommendations = 5+ from clients (not just colleagues).
**Key Takeaway:** A buyer-optimized profile converts profile visits into sales conversations — a rep's best asset.

### Post 5 — Content That Helps Your Sales Team Close
**Topic:** Sales enablement through LinkedIn content
**Summary:** Sam argues that marketing content on LinkedIn should be designed to help sales reps share it in their pipeline. The best LinkedIn posts for sales teams are: ones that address common sales objections publicly, case studies that prospects can share internally, and thought leadership that makes the rep look credible to their buyers. She calls this "pipeline-aware content."
**Key Takeaway:** Market LinkedIn content that your sales team can use in deals — pipeline-aware content closes deals faster.

### Post 6 — Why Most B2B Cold Outreach Fails on LinkedIn
**Topic:** LinkedIn DM strategy for B2B
**Summary:** Sam shares the top 5 LinkedIn DM mistakes that guarantee no reply: (1) connecting with no personalized note, (2) pitching in the first message, (3) using the same template for every prospect, (4) sending a wall of text, (5) not following up. She provides her 3-step DM sequence that gets 30%+ reply rates.
**Key Takeaway:** Never pitch in the first LinkedIn DM — establish genuine interest before asking for anything.

### Post 7 — The Revenue Impact of a Seller's Personal Brand
**Topic:** Measuring LinkedIn ROI for sales teams
**Summary:** Sam shares data from #samsales clients: reps who post on LinkedIn 3×/week generate 25% more pipeline than reps who don't — same territory, same ICP, same product. The mechanism: content builds credibility → credibility shortens the trust-building phase → trust shortens the sales cycle. She argues every B2B SaaS company should have a LinkedIn personal brand program for its sales team.
**Key Takeaway:** Seller personal brands generate 25% more pipeline for the same territory — the ROI is measurable.
