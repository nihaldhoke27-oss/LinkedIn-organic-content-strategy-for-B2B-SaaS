## Short Bio

Dave Gerhardt is the founder of Exit Five, a B2B marketing community and media brand, and former CMO at Drift (scaled from Series A to $1B+ valuation) and Privy. He is widely regarded as one of the architects of modern B2B brand marketing — specifically the shift from corporate, feature-led content to personality-driven, opinionated B2B voices. His Exit Five community has 5,000+ B2B marketing practitioners and is one of the most active B2B marketing communities globally. His LinkedIn presence is a masterclass in anti-corporate B2B brand voice.

## Writing Patterns & Style Analysis

### Hook Style
Dave uses direct, often provocative, peer-to-peer openers:
- **Rant/opinion hook:** "I'm tired of seeing B2B companies write content nobody wants to read."
- **Personal story:** "I made a career mistake that cost me a CMO role. Here's what I learned."
- **Contrarian take:** "B2B marketing doesn't need more blogs. It needs more opinions."
- **Question as hook:** "How many of you are running campaigns your own team doesn't believe in?"

### Post Structure
Dave's posts read like CMO journal entries:
1. **Raw observation or personal story**
2. **The lesson (stated plainly, without hedging)**
3. **Why it matters for B2B SaaS marketers specifically**
4. **Optional: Community question to generate comments**
5. **Soft CTA (rarely hard-sell)**

### Formatting Habits
- Short, punchy sentences — sounds like how he'd actually talk
- Frequent use of "Look:" and "Here's the thing:" as pivots
- Uses line breaks generously but not as mechanically as Justin Welsh
- Occasionally uses mild profanity for authenticity ("I don't care about..." / "This is BS.")
- Rarely uses bullet lists — prefers narrative prose
- Posts feel personal, not polished

### CTA Style
- **Community CTAs:** "Come talk about this in the Exit Five community — link in bio."
- **Podcast CTAs:** "Covered this on the Exit Five pod this week. Episode link in comments."
- **Newsletter CTAs:** "Exit Five Weekly drops every Thursday. Join 40K+ B2B marketers."
- **Debate CTAs:** "Agree? Disagree? Tell me below — I want to hear the other side."

## Recent LinkedIn Post Summaries

> ⚠️ **Note:** LinkedIn does not allow public post scraping. Summaries are based on publicly referenced posts, Exit Five newsletter content, podcast episodes, and shared content on other platforms.

### Post 1 — The Best B2B Marketing is Human
**Topic:** B2B brand voice and authenticity
**Summary:** Dave argues that the most successful B2B companies of the last decade (Drift, Gong, Slack, HubSpot) all share one trait: their content sounds like a person wrote it, not a committee approved it. He argues that "safe" corporate content is actually the riskiest content strategy because it generates no differentiation and no emotional response.
**Key Takeaway:** In B2B, the most dangerous content strategy is being boring and safe.
**Hook:** "The most dangerous thing you can do in B2B marketing is be boring."

### Post 2 — CMO Lessons from Drift
**Topic:** B2B brand building case study
**Summary:** Dave shares 3 lessons from building Drift's brand from Series A to unicorn: (1) the CEO must be the chief content creator, (2) pick a contrarian position and defend it publicly (Drift's anti-form narrative), (3) build community before product launches. He argues these principles apply to any B2B SaaS company, not just Drift.
**Key Takeaway:** B2B brand is built through consistent, opinionated content — not ad spend.

### Post 3 — The Marketing-Sales Alignment Gap
**Topic:** Marketing accountability for revenue
**Summary:** Dave shares his view that marketing's primary job is to make sales conversations easier — not generate MQLs. He argues the best B2B marketing creates "pre-sold" prospects: buyers who have consumed enough content that they come into sales calls already believing in the product's value. LinkedIn organic content is his primary tool for creating pre-sold prospects.
**Key Takeaway:** Your content should make your sales team's job easier. If it doesn't, it's failing.

### Post 4 — Why Every B2B Company Needs an Exit Five
**Topic:** Community-led growth case study
**Summary:** Dave shares the Exit Five growth story: started as a Slack group, grew to 5,000+ paying members, generates revenue through memberships + sponsorships + events. He argues B2B SaaS companies should build their own communities before buying ads — because communities create loyalty that ads cannot.
**Key Takeaway:** A community of 1,000 deeply engaged buyers is worth more than 100,000 passive social followers.

### Post 5 — LinkedIn Posts for CMOs Who Hate LinkedIn
**Topic:** LinkedIn strategy for senior B2B marketers
**Summary:** Dave addresses the CMO who says "I don't have time for LinkedIn." He reframes it: your LinkedIn profile is your company's highest-converting demand gen asset. He argues that 30 minutes/day on LinkedIn builds more credibility than any campaign budget, because trust is built at the individual level, not the brand level.
**Key Takeaway:** 30 min/day on LinkedIn as a CMO generates more trust than any campaign budget.

### Post 6 — The Newsletter Every B2B Team Should Publish
**Topic:** B2B newsletter strategy
**Summary:** Dave argues that a company newsletter (sent weekly, under 500 words, written by a real person) is the highest-ROI marketing asset in B2B SaaS. He shares that Exit Five Weekly generates more qualified pipeline per subscriber than any other Exit Five marketing channel.
**Key Takeaway:** Weekly newsletter + LinkedIn = the minimum viable B2B marketing stack for 2025.

### Post 7 — What B2B Marketing Gets Wrong About Brand
**Topic:** B2B brand investment
**Summary:** Dave shares his framework for brand investment: brand is anything that makes a buyer think of you before they're in the market. LinkedIn organic content is brand. Podcasts are brand. Community is brand. Paid ads are lead capture, not brand. He argues most SaaS marketing teams have the budget allocation backwards.
**Key Takeaway:** Invest in brand before you need leads. Brand is the pre-condition for efficient lead capture.

## Key Takeaways for B2B SaaS Strategy

1. **Sound like a human, not a company** — anti-corporate brand voice is a differentiator.
2. **CEO = chief content creator** — the founder's LinkedIn is the company's most valuable marketing asset.
3. **Build community before you need customers** — community creates pre-sold prospects.
4. **Content should make sales easier** — pre-sold prospects close faster and at higher ACV.
5. **Brand investment precedes efficient lead capture** — you can't harvest demand you haven't created.
6. **Weekly newsletter + LinkedIn = minimum viable B2B marketing stack.**
7. **Pick a contrarian position and defend it** — safe content generates no differentiation.
